## 3. Patterns worth noting

### Margin hierarchy

| Business | Typical OP margin |
|---|---|
| **Monopoly niche equipment** (Lasertec, ASML) | **45–50%** |
| **Leading-edge foundry** (TSMC) | 45% |
| **Specialty WFE** (KLA process control, Advantest HBM test) | 28–35% |
| **Broad-line WFE** (AMAT, Lam, ASM) | 28–30% |
| **Fabless SoC** (MediaTek) | 19% |
| **Mature foundry** (UMC) | 22% |
| **Specialty foundry** (GFS) | ~15–20% (implied) |
| **ATE test** (Teradyne — current) | 22% (target: 30–34%) |
| **OSAT** (ASE standalone pkg+test) | ~11% segment; 6.6% blended incl. EMS |
| **Memory OSAT** (ChipMOS) | ~6–8% |
| **Low-end distribution / EMS** | 2–5% |

The wider the moat — *and* the more specialized the tool — the fatter the margin. The single highest-margin business in this entire table is Lasertec's EUV mask inspection. The single lowest-margin is EMS (inside ASE).

### Growth trajectories in FY2024–FY2025

| Tier | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Explosive (+40–60%)** — AI/HBM-geared | Advantest (+60.3%), Lasertec (+17.8% realized but +60% peak on orders since reversed), Kokusai Electric (+32%) |
| **Strong (+20–30%)** — foundry + leading-edge exposure | TSMC (+30%), MediaTek (+22.4%), KLA (+24%) |
| **Flattish (0–5%)** — diversified/mature-node exposure | ASE (+2.3%), AMAT (+4.4%) |
| **Declining or one-time-hit** | Nikon (-5.6% + ¥100B impairment) |

### The CapEx / R&D signal

- **TSMC R&D ~7% of revenue** — this is the "tax" that every leading-edge foundry pays. It's the highest R&D ratio among the foundries and is what funds the N2/A16 roadmap.
- **Kokusai R&D 6.5%, AMAT ~7%** — WFE R&D ratios are similar to foundries; it's an R&D-intensive industry.
- **Teradyne R&D 15.8% of revenue** — unusually high because ATE R&D is a fixed-ish cost that gets amortized over a cyclical top line; when revenue is in a trough, R&D % looks high.
- **ASE CapEx 3.2% of revenue** — deceptively low because EMS is capex-light; the semi-pkg&test piece has CapEx-to-revenue closer to 10%.
- **ChipMOS CapEx 24% of revenue** — intensely reinvesting, which is why OSATs get operating leverage once cycles turn.

### Geographic / China exposure

- **KLA 89% international revenue** — global but China-heavy historically; the 20% RPO decline hints at order compression partly from China export controls.
- **AMAT** China exposure cut from ~30%+ down toward mid-teens over 2023–2025 — the clearest public evidence of US export controls reshaping revenue mix.
- **Ebara 70% overseas** — but infrastructure segment is geographically different from semi segment.

### What's cyclical vs. structural

A careful read of these reports shows a **bifurcated market in 2024–2025**:
- **Structural winners:** HBM test (Advantest), HBM probe cards (Micronics — not in this table), ALD (Kokusai, ASM), CoWoS-feeders (all packaging names), leading-edge foundry (TSMC).
- **Cyclical cooling:** EUV mask inspection orders (Lasertec — orders -60%), mature-node China exposure (several US WFE names), litho (Nikon impairments).

This split shows up in Lasertec and KLA's backlog numbers *despite* record realized revenue — the industry is a cycle inside a cycle, with AI demand riding on top of a broader WFE trough.
