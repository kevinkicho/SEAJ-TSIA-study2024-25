# Gaps & Prioritized Reading List

*What your current folder doesn't cover — and in what order to fill the gaps. Your collection is unusually strong on Japan equipment + Taiwan manufacturing, but it has structural blind spots that make certain questions unanswerable until you add the missing players.*

---

## How to read this

Each gap is scored:

- **Priority** — 1 = essential (you cannot form a complete view without this); 2 = strongly recommended; 3 = useful when you go deeper
- **Why** — what question this unlocks
- **Start with** — a specific first document to get you 80% of the value

---

## Priority 1 — Essential (fill these first)

### 1. ASML Holding (Netherlands)

**Status in your folder:** missing entirely.
**Why it's the biggest hole:** ASML is the **only company on earth that makes EUV scanners**. Every TSMC N5/N3/N2 wafer starts on an ASML TWINSCAN. Without it you cannot explain leading-edge economics, US–China export controls, TSMC capex cycles, or why Lasertec/Zeiss matter.
**Start with:** 2024 Annual Report → read the "Technology" section (EUV, High-NA EUV, DUV backlog) and the geographic revenue split (notice the China % shift over 2022→2025).
**Connects to what you have:** Lasertec (inspects ASML's masks), Zeiss (makes ASML's optics), JSR/Shin-Etsu (resist/wafer), TSMC (buys the tools).

### 2. Samsung Electronics — Semiconductor Division (Korea)

**Status:** missing.
**Why:** Samsung is simultaneously #1 memory (DRAM + NAND), #2 foundry, and a customer for most of your Japan equipment companies. Its capex swings drive 10–15% of the WFE market.
**Start with:** Samsung Electronics 2024 Business Report / 10-K equivalent (DART filing, English). Focus on DS (Device Solutions) segment disclosures.
**Connects to:** competes with TSMC; buys from AMAT/Lam/KLA/TEL/Ebara/Advantest; sells HBM to NVIDIA.

### 3. SK Hynix (Korea)

**Status:** missing — and given your HBM/GPU file, this is the biggest thematic gap.
**Why:** **SK Hynix is the #1 HBM supplier** (~50% share through HBM3E) and the sole HBM supplier to NVIDIA through most of 2024. Understanding AI-driven chip demand without SK Hynix is like studying smartphones without Apple.
**Start with:** SK Hynix 2024 Annual Report + the Q4 2024 earnings deck.
**Connects to:** Advantest (HBM test monopoly — so SK Hynix is Advantest's biggest tailwind), Micronics (HBM probe cards), TSMC (CoWoS integrates SK Hynix HBM), NVIDIA (end customer).

### 4. Micron Technology (US)

**Status:** missing.
**Why:** The only US-listed pure-play memory company. Useful for US-disclosure quality (10-K/10-Q detail) on DRAM/NAND economics. Also a HBM3E ramp entrant.
**Start with:** Micron FY2024 10-K.
**Connects to:** same memory stack as SK Hynix/Samsung; buys from Lam/AMAT/TEL.

### 5. NVIDIA (US)

**Status:** missing (you have Intel Core Ultra brief, which is a different market).
**Why:** NVIDIA is now the demand-side gravity of the entire AI build-out — ~70% of TSMC's CoWoS capacity in 2024/25 served NVIDIA. Reading NVIDIA's 10-K / investor day closes the loop: fab → OSAT → HBM → GPU → hyperscaler.
**Start with:** NVIDIA FY2025 10-K (fiscal year ends January) + the most recent GTC keynote transcript.
**Connects to:** basically everything else in your folder.

### 6. Tokyo Electron (TEL) (Japan) — `8035.T`

**Status:** missing. **Biggest Japanese gap.**
**Why:** TEL is the **#3 or #4 WFE company globally** (after AMAT and ASML, often swapping with Lam). Dominant in coater/developer (near-monopoly, pairs with every ASML scanner), plus etch, cleans, wafer probers. You cannot map SEAJ without TEL — it's the SEAJ heavyweight.
**Start with:** TEL FY3/2025 Integrated Report → "Segment Information" + "Market Environment".
**Connects to:** ASML (coater/developer sold with every scanner), JSR (uses TEL track with JSR resist), TSMC/Samsung/SK Hynix (customers).

### 7. Synopsys + Siemens EDA (+ deeper Cadence)

**Status:** you have one Cadence marketing asset; no Synopsys/Siemens.
**Why:** EDA is a 3-firm oligopoly (Synopsys, Cadence, Siemens EDA ex-Mentor). Every chip in your folder was designed with their tools. The industry's hidden toll-taker.
**Start with:** Synopsys FY2024 10-K + Cadence FY2024 10-K. Compare revenue mix (IP vs. tools vs. systems), recurring revenue %, and AI-tool positioning.

## Priority 2 — Strongly recommended

### 8. Kioxia (Japan NAND)

Why: NAND #3 or #4; JV partner to Western Digital (now Sandisk-revived). A pure Japanese memory play — complements your Japan-heavy collection.
Start with: Kioxia FY3/2025 Annual Report (re-IPO'd late 2024).

### 9. SMIC (Semiconductor Manufacturing International Corp) — China

Why: China's leading-edge foundry. Understanding US export controls requires the counterparty.
Start with: SMIC 2024 Annual Report (HKEX filing, English) + the 7nm/N+2 discussions in analyst calls.

### 10. Naura Technology + AMEC (China WFE)

Why: Naura (etch, PVD, CVD) and AMEC (etch, MOCVD) are the Chinese WFE national champions — the reason US WFE exports to China are collapsing. For a balanced picture of global WFE share you need them.
Start with: Naura 2024 annual report + AMEC 2024 annual report (both Shanghai STAR Market, English available).

### 11. Infineon + STMicroelectronics + NXP + onsemi (power & analog)

Why: You have Fuji Electric and AMS OSRAM, but the global auto-semi top 4 is Infineon / NXP / STMicro / Renesas. Auto and industrial MCU is a huge piece of the industry — largely absent from your folder.
Start with: Infineon FY2024 Annual Report (fiscal year ends September) — the "Automotive / Green Industrial Power" segmentation is a great template.

### 12. Renesas (Japan) — `6723.T`

Why: Japan's #1 MCU maker. Auto MCU + industrial. Rounds out Japan IDM coverage.
Start with: Renesas 2024 Integrated Report.

### 13. Sony Semiconductor Solutions (Japan)

Why: **~50% of global image sensor market** (every iPhone camera). A pure-play CIS giant. You have AMS OSRAM on the sensor side but not the market leader.
Start with: Sony FY2024 Group Annual Report → Imaging & Sensing Solutions segment; or the SSS investor day deck.

### 14. TSMC 2025 Q1–Q4 earnings packs + CoWoS capacity commentary

Why: Your TSMC annual report is 2024; the story changed materially through 2025 (CoWoS-L ramp, 2nm pilot, Arizona/Japan/Germany fab progress). Supplement the annual with quarterly calls.
Start with: TSMC Q4 2024 + Q4 2025 earnings presentations.

### 15. Intel Foundry (IFS) disclosure

Why: You have a Core Ultra product brief. Intel as a **foundry** (18A node) is separate — the third leading-edge player besides TSMC and Samsung.
Start with: Intel FY2024 10-K → new "Intel Foundry" segment disclosure (started 2024).

### 16. SEMI (industry body) — WFE market tracker

Why: SEMI publishes the authoritative WFE spending forecast and equipment segmentation. Monthly billings report + yearly outlook are the industry pulse.
Start with: SEMI "World Fab Forecast" (subscription) — or the free quarterly "Year-End Total Equipment Forecast" press release.

### 17. SUMCO (Japan) — `3436.T`

Why: #2 silicon wafer maker (you have Shin-Etsu = #1). Duopoly, so you need both.
Start with: SUMCO FY12/2024 Annual Report.

### 18. Tokyo Ohka Kogyo (TOK) + Fujifilm Electronic Materials + Shin-Etsu Chemical's resist

Why: Photoresist market completion. You have JSR; TOK is the other pure-play.
Start with: TOK 2024 Integrated Report.

### 19. Entegris (US) — fab chemicals/materials delivery

Why: Critical consumables (specialty gases, CMP slurry distribution, filtration). Neutral US supplier to every fab.
Start with: Entegris FY2024 10-K.

### 20. Tokyo Elec Device / Macnica-Fuji (JP distributors)

Why: Complement WPG in the distribution layer with Japan-side distributors.
Start with: Macnica Holdings 2024 Integrated Report.

## Priority 3 — Deepening / specialty

### 21. BE Semiconductor (Besi) — Netherlands

Start with: Besi 2024 Annual Report.

### 22. EV Group (Austria) + SUSS MicroTec (Germany)

Why: Wafer bonding & lithography for advanced packaging and compound semi.
Start with: SUSS FY2024 Annual Report.

### 23. ASE's peers you're missing: Amkor (US) + JCET (China) + Powertech (Taiwan)

Why: You have ASE + ChipMOS + Sigurd + KYEC. Amkor (#2 globally) and JCET (China) complete the global OSAT top-5. Powertech is TW-memory-OSAT specialist.
Start with: Amkor FY2024 10-K.

### 24. GlobalWafers (TW) — `6488.TW`

Why: #3 silicon wafer maker (behind Shin-Etsu and SUMCO). Taiwan-listed.
Start with: GlobalWafers 2024 annual report.

### 25. Rapidus (Japan) — 2nm foundry attempt

Why: State-backed Japanese effort to leapfrog back into leading-edge. Even if skeptical, reading their materials shows Japan's industrial-policy logic.
Start with: Rapidus' corporate brochure + METI's "Semiconductor and Digital Industry Strategy".

### 26. WOLFSPEED (US) — SiC power

Why: Pure-play silicon carbide. Bellwether for EV/industrial SiC demand. With Fuji Electric, rounds out power semi.
Start with: Wolfspeed FY2024 10-K.

### 27. Keysight + Anritsu + Rohde & Schwarz (test & measurement adjacency)

Why: Test-and-measurement beyond ATE — you have Advantest & Teradyne. Keysight (US) and Anritsu (JP) cover RF/protocol test.
Start with: Keysight FY2024 10-K.

### 28. Applied Materials' subsidiary names and Lam's specific products

Why: Tool-level fluency. Knowing that "Endura" = AMAT PVD platform, "Centura" = etch/CVD, "Producer" = CVD; and "Kiyo" = Lam etch, "VECTOR" = Lam ALD — unlocks the equipment-level literature.
Start with: SEMI's process-flow handbook or any industry-standard semi process text (e.g., Quirk & Serda).

### 29. Macro/policy

- **EU Chips Act** — TSMC Dresden JV, Intel Magdeburg, Infineon expansion.

- **KR K-CHIPS** — SK Hynix Yongin cluster.
- **CN "Big Fund"** — Phase 3 ($47B), SMIC support.

Start with: SEMI "Government Funding Tracker" (quarterly update) — single best aggregator.

## Cross-reference: what your folder uniquely gives you that a generalist wouldn't have

A balanced view: don't discount what you already have. These are collection strengths that most US-centric readers *don't* have access to:

- **Japanese integrated reports** (Lasertec, Disco, Accretech, Ebara, Shin-Etsu, JSR) — Japanese disclosure quality on long-term R&D, share data by product, and long-range segment trend-lines is often *better* than US 10-Ks.
- **Taiwan annual reports in English** (TSMC, UMC, MediaTek, ASE, ChipMOS) — TWSE annual reports include detailed shareholder/ownership tables, related-party transactions, and ultra-detailed segmentation that's rare elsewhere.
- **Niche SEAJ members** (Marumae, Micronics Japan, Nuflare, Rikken Keiki) — tiny companies but with monopoly positions. The English materials you have are the *only* way to access these companies unless you read Japanese.
- **Distribution-layer visibility** (WPG, Topco, Nagase, Marketech) — distributor reports reveal end-market inventory pulses earlier than chip-maker reports.

Keep this in mind: you're not missing "the obvious stuff," you're missing "the US/Korea/EU equivalents of what you already have in JP/TW." The gap is symmetrical, not uneven.
